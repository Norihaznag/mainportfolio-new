## 1. Morocco vs Abroad

### Do NOT run ads targeting Morocco

**Why:**
- Moroccan businesses expect to pay 2,000–5,000 MAD ($200-500) for a full website. Your cheapest package is $997.
- Very few Moroccan startups have the budget for custom software.
- Payment collection is harder (no Stripe in Morocco, bank transfers are slow).
- The market is small — 37 million people, most not in your ICP.
- CPM (cost per 1,000 views) in Morocco is cheap (≈$1-3), but the leads are low quality.

### The ONE exception for Morocco:
- If you offer a **cheap local package** (e.g., 5,000 MAD website for local businesses — restaurants, clinics, lawyers). This is a completely different business and dilutes your brand. I'd avoid it.

### Target abroad. Here's why:
- Your prices ($997-$4,997) are normal/cheap by Western standards.
- Western clients pay on time via Stripe/PayPal/Wise.
- The pool of startup founders is 100x larger.
- You can justify higher prices because of quality, not geography.
- A $2,500 project = ~25,000 MAD. That's a great month in Morocco.

**Verdict: 100% target abroad. Zero ads in Morocco.**

## 4. Campaign Types Ranked by ROI

From best to worst return on your money:

### 1. 🏆 Retargeting website visitors (BEST ROI)
- **What:** Show ads to people who already visited azinag.site
- **Why it works:** They already know you — just need a nudge
- **Cost per lead:** $5-15
- **Setup:** Requires Meta Pixel (✅ you have this)
- **Budget:** $3-5/day

### 2. 🥈 Cold audience — interest + job title targeting
- **What:** Target startup founders/CEOs by interest and job title on Facebook/Instagram
- **Why it works:** Reaches the right people, relatively affordable
- **Cost per lead:** $15-50
- **Budget:** $5-10/day

### 3. 🥉 Lookalike audience (after 100+ site visitors)
- **What:** Meta finds people similar to your site visitors
- **Why it works:** Algorithm is incredibly good at finding similar people
- **Cost per lead:** $10-30
- **Requirement:** Need 100+ pixel events first (wait ~2-4 weeks)
- **Budget:** $5-10/day

### 4. LinkedIn Ads (expensive but precise)
- **What:** Target by exact job title + company size
- **Why it works:** No wasted impressions — every viewer is a potential client
- **Cost per lead:** $30-100
- **Problem:** Minimum $10/day, clicks cost $8-15 each
- **Budget:** Only if you have $450+/month to spare

### 5. Google Search Ads (high intent but competitive)
- **What:** Show up when someone googles "hire developer for MVP"
- **Why it works:** These people are actively looking to buy
- **Cost per lead:** $20-80
- **Problem:** Competitive keywords, needs landing page optimization
- **Budget:** $10-15/day minimum to get data

**My recommendation: Start with #1 (retargeting) + #2 (cold audience) only. Ignore the rest until month 3.**

├── Placements: Automatic
├── Optimization: Landing page views

├── Format: Single image OR short video (30-60 sec screen recording)

### Important settings:
- **Always use "Landing page views"** as optimization, not "Link clicks" — landing page views filter out accidental clicks

- **Start with Automatic placements** — let Meta decide Instagram vs Facebook vs Stories vs Reels

## 7. Rules to NOT Waste Money

### Rule 1: The $20 test
Never spend more than $20 on an ad variation without checking results. If CTR (click-through rate) is below 1% after $20 spent → kill it.

### Rule 2: Test 3 ads, kill 2
Always run 3 versions of the same ad (different headline or image). After 3 days, keep only the winner. Run 2 new challengers against it.

### Rule 3: Don't touch for 3 days
After launching an ad, **do not change anything for 72 hours**. Meta's algorithm needs time to learn. Editing resets the learning phase.

### Rule 4: Budget changes = max 20%
If you want to increase budget, do it in 20% increments. Going from $5 to $15 overnight resets the algorithm. Go $5 → $6 → $7.50 → $9 etc.

### Rule 5: One variable at a time
Testing a new image? Keep the copy the same. Testing new copy? Keep the image. Otherwise you don't know what worked.

### Rule 6: Check at the same time every day
Pick a time (e.g., 9 AM). Check metrics once per day. Don't check every hour — it leads to panic decisions.

### Rule 7: Landing page matters more than the ad
A great ad sending traffic to a bad landing page = wasted money. Make sure azinag.site/lp/startup-mvp-development loads fast, has social proof, and has a clear CTA.

### Rule 8: Weekend ads are cheaper
CPMs drop 20-40% on weekends. In some cases, keep ads running 7 days but know that weekend performance may look different.

### Rule 9: Never boost posts from your page
Always create ads through **Ads Manager** (business.facebook.com). The "Boost" button on your Facebook page gives you zero control and wastes money.

### Rule 10: Track EVERYTHING
Use UTM parameters on every ad link:
```
azinag.site/lp/startup-mvp-development?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=cold-us-uk&utm_content=ad-v1
```
This shows up in Google Analytics so you know exactly which ad brought which visitor.

## 8. When to Scale, When to Kill

### Key metrics to watch:

| Metric | Good | Bad — action needed |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| **CTR (click-through rate)** | >1.5% | <0.8% → change creative |
| **CPC (cost per click)** | <$1.50 | >$3 → narrow targeting or change ad |
| **CPL (cost per lead/contact)** | <$30 | >$60 → review landing page |
| **Bounce rate (GA4)** | <60% | >80% → landing page problem |
| **Frequency** | <3 | >5 → audience is tired of your ad, make new one |

### Decision framework:

```
After 3 days & $15-20 spent:
├── CTR > 1.5%, CPC < $1.50 → ✅ KEEP, let it run 7 more days
├── CTR 0.8-1.5%, CPC $1.50-3 → ⚠️ WAIT 3 more days, watch closely
├── CTR < 0.8%, CPC > $3 → ❌ KILL, test new creative
└── Getting clicks but zero contacts → 🔧 FIX landing page, not the ad

After 7 days of a winning ad:
├── Got a lead/contact? → ✅ Increase budget by 20%
├── No leads despite good clicks → 🔧 Fix contact page or add more social proof
└── Leads but nobody books a call → 🔧 Follow up faster (within 1 hour)

After 1 month:
├── CAC < $500 with avg project $2,500 → 🚀 SCALE to next phase
├── CAC $500-1000 → ⚠️ Optimize before scaling
└── CAC > $1000 → ⛔ Pause ads, focus on organic + referrals
```

### When to increase budget:
- You've had at least 1 paying client from ads
- Your CAC is below 20% of your average project value
- The winning ad has been stable for 7+ days

### When to pause everything:
- You've spent $300 with zero contact form submissions
- You don't have time to respond to leads within 2 hours
- You're getting leads but can't handle the workload

## 9. Free Alternatives to Ads (Do These First)

Before spending a single dirham, max out these free channels:

### LinkedIn outreach (30 min/day)
1. Search for "startup founder" or "CEO" in your target countries
2. Send 10-15 connection requests/day with a personal note:
   > "Hey [Name], saw you're building [their startup]. Really cool concept. I build MVPs for startup founders — happy to connect."
3. After they accept, **don't pitch immediately**. Engage with their posts for a week, then DM.
4. **Expected result:** 5-10 conversations/week → 1-2 calls/month

### Twitter/X engagement (20 min/day)
1. Follow startup founders, indie hackers, and people posting about building products
2. Reply to their tweets with genuine, helpful comments (not "nice!" — add value)
3. Quote-tweet with your perspective
4. **Expected result:** 50-100 new followers/week → inbound DMs

### Product Hunt & Indie Hackers
1. Comment on launches related to your expertise
2. Post in Indie Hackers asking founders what they struggle with in development
3. Be helpful first, pitch never — people will check your profile

### Cold email (advanced — month 2+)
1. Find startup founders on LinkedIn/Crunchbase who recently raised funding
2. Send a personalized email (NOT a template):
   > "Hey [Name], congrats on the [round]. I noticed [their product] doesn't have [feature]. I build exactly that kind of thing — shipped a similar feature for [client] in 3 weeks. Want me to send a quick scope?"
3. Use Lemlist or Instantly.ai ($30-50/month) for sequences
4. **Expected result:** 2-5% reply rate → 1-2 calls per 100 emails
